6.Why is it necessary to conserve biodiversity?
Ans.
The reasons for conserving biodiversity can be grouped into three categories.
i) Narrow utilitarian reasons:- Human beings derive a number of economic benefits like
food, fibre,firewood, industrial product & medicinal products.
ii) Broad utilitarian reasons:- Biodiversity plays a major role in providing ecosystem services
like :-
a) production of oxygen
b) Pollination of flowers, without which seeds or fruits are not produced.
c) Aesthetic pleasures like bird watching, watching spring flowers, walking through thick
forest, workingup to bulbulâ€™s song etc.
iii) Ethical reasons :- Every species has an intrinsic value even if it is not of any economic
value to us-wehave a moral duty to care for their well-being & pass on the biological legacy
in a proper from to ourfuture generation.
