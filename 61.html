6. What does GIFT represent?
Ans.
It is the introduction of two unfertilized oocytes and several sperms into the fallopian
tube of a woman desirous to be a mother through laproscope. The eggs may be hers or a
donor’s. The sperms may be of her husband’s or of a donor. Fertilisation occurs in vivo and
the development of the foetus takes place through natural process.
7. How does Cu- T act as a contraceptive?
Ans.
It is an intrauterine device having ionized copper. The copper diffuses into the uterus
and brings about the release of toxic cytokines. They inhibit sperm motility and therefore
fertilization of ovum.
8. Mention any four probable reasons for the rapid rise of population in our country?
Ans.
The probable reasons could be:
Steady decline in the death rate due to improved health services.
Early marriages especially in certain rural areas.
Lack of education among the poor and they fail to understand the ill effects of a large
family.
Longer life span.
